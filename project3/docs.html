Music Time

Original Concept

A little 2d music experience where the user can click on certain objects for them to make sounds at a chosen interval, akin to my singing monsters or other online music creation platforms.

Asethethics

2d pixel graphics most likely. Sounds used will be very simple and probably be common chiptune sounds.

Genre

Music

Platform

Desktop only

Gameplay

Player will make music using the sounds I provide by setting intervals between beats and length of beats and any other interactions I can think of.

Process

What do I make?

I started originally with an idea for a little 2d pixel art dungeon crawler utilizing the pixiJS wrapper but after deliberating a little bit and thinking of other ideas I decided to make a little music site. The primary inspiration for this site is music creation sites like beepbox or incredibox. My original mockup looked a lot more like the game My singing monsters, developed by Big Blue Bubble, but I ended up with a design that is more reminiscent of a MPC or music production controller.

How do I make this?

The first thing that I did was grab a couple of test sounds off of the internet as placeholders. With those in place I then tried my best to create a scalable system with my initial test sounds. I decided that the best way to handle that was by using arrays of objects that contained all the adjustable settings of the howler js wrapper. After that it was just a matter of matching the correct sound to the correct object in the array and then changing the properties of that sound.

Endgame

One issue that I noticed when interacting with my site was that having to select sounds by hovering over them and then navigating through the maze of other sounds in the way was tedious, so after fidgeting with the code and thinking a way to make the process less tedious I decided to add keyboard events to my site. With keyboard events it allows the user to use their keyboard to choose the sounds that they want to edit. Though I didn't add a button to allow the user to play the sound from the keyboard too the implementation of the keyboard events made it so the sounds could be edited and then even if the user mouses over other buttons to play the sound they want to it will have no adverse effect because they will end up hovering over the button they wanted to play in the end.
